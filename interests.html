Model UN

NB: This section is long, but rightfully so as MUN has been a huge part of my life

I've been actively involved in Model UN since my freshman year of high school, traveling to over a dozen collegiate conferences with the rest of my High School MUN team. These conferences are grueling, 4-day-long negotiation and public speaking marathons, and through them I've made some of my strongest friendships. Among my favorite conferences were BUSUN (Brown University), JUMUNC (Johns Hopkins), and WAMUNC (Goerge Washington University) in High School, and BARMUN (Boston University) and CMUNNY (Columbia University) in college.

During the 2021 academic year, I served as Head Delegate of the NYU Model UN Travel Team. As Head Delegate, I planned and conducted weekly practice - two hours every Monday to keep our speaking and networking skills sharp - and represented our delegation when we competed virtually. We now compete in-person due to COVID restrictions being lifted.

Aside from competing as a delegate, I also have been involved in planning and staffing Model UN events since my freshman year of High School. MUN conferences can have anywhere from 50 to 500+ attendees, and can last up to four days, so coordinating staffing, logistics, and content for these conferences is - in mathematical terms - nontrivial .

This year I served as Under Secretary-General for Delegate Affairs for NYUMUNC XIV, NYU's collegiate conference. As the point person for Delegate Affairs, I was responsible for all things pertaining to attendees of the conference. This meant:

  1. Managing invoicing and payment through NYU for the 20+ schools attending.
  2. Assigning 200+ delegates between our 16 committees, and keeping track of them day-of.
  3. All communication with attendees (i.e. Sending SO MANY emails).
  4. Being the first to respond if and when something goes wrong.

I can now finally say that I am proficient in Excel.
